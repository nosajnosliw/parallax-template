DESIGNING FOR PHYSICAL INTERACTONS

AV&C is an award winning A/V consultancy that specializes in designing interactive systems and their controls, sourcing and installing the necessary hardware, developing the custom software and integrating these interactive installation of this into existing and new constructions.

My role as the Software Development Production Coordinator focused on managing the software development for the experiential real estate sales centers and generative LED displays. In the absence of a UI designer, I was able to volunteer myself in order to seize an opportunity to grow as a designer

The first sales center UI that I worked on was a bit clunky but I was able to learn a lot from this. After collaborating with our Principal Designer and Project Manager on the basic UI flow, I set out sketching.

This sales center also featured an interactive touch table display which I also was responsible for creating the annotated wireframes for.

SECOND TRY

We'd received a lot of client feedback from the first sales center. This praise and criticism, along with lessons learned from the multiple iteration processes helped me to streamline the design for the next sales center. I was also more aware of the modules that were pretty much identical to the first, so the time it took to concept and reiterate was much shorter.

Unfortunately, tying the physical experience to the iPad UI and data model were much more difficult on this project as the marketing design for the physical experience really didn't take into mind how everything would correlate to the tablet UI prompts.

ALMOST RIGHT

The third sales center gave me an opportunity to really think hard about the way that I would approach the design, and allowed me to collaborate even greater with our marketing partner and our dev team to tighten it up. This being the third go round with this particular partner, I had grown very close to the digital and project management team and was able to share client feedback and also some ideas about how to elevate the design. CURVEBALL: CMS UX

One very challenging project was creating a wireframe for the show control CMS for an extensive interactive LED display installations. LED lighting strips installed on the exterior of Seattle's Columbia Center tower, would illuminate and animate in coordination with events scheduled in the custom CMS built by our dev team. Our dev team specifically requested this deliverable in an effort to grasp the complexity of the tool that we had been commissioned to build. So I teamed up with our Experience Director, Software Developer and Project Manager and started Graffling.

MATERIAL WORLD

I'm a lifelong learner, and I'm always soaking up new information, especially regarding development and design. So when our dev team suggested that we steer our direction to the Google Material design methodology for the next sales center, I immediately hit the books, or well Google. After soaking up as much as I could, as per usual the pen hit the paper.

After a week or so more of rigorous study, I armed myself with a comprehensive UI kit and set to work putting together my PSD experiment based off of some of the experiences that we had already worked on. Click to see the entire set of frames.

One problem we noticed on these jobs is that the not being responsible for the final UI visual design negatively impacted our UX. Experimenting with this new higher fidelity work flow showed me that we could cut down wireframe and UI reiteration time between our teams. This workflow would also allow for greater influence on the final UI to make sure that it adhered to our UX.
